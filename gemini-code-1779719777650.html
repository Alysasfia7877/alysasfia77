Optimizing Rice Production Yields via Derivatives

Analyzing the marginal efficiency of nitrogen fertilizer application on local MR297 paddy variants in Kedah (The Rice Bowl of Malaysia).

The Bioscience Problem

To ensure national self-sufficiency and food security, Malaysian paddy farmers must maximize grain yield while mitigating chemical runoff. Using a quadratic response model common in crop science, this simulation charts the total yield of MR297 rice based on nitrogen fertilizer inputs.

By utilizing the first derivative $f'(x)$, we calculate the marginal yield change. This shows the exact efficiency of every single kilogram of fertilizer added, identifying the point of diminishing returns and the absolute optimal input level to eliminate waste.

Data Webpage Baseline: Integrated from MARDI (Malaysian Agricultural Research and Development Institute) Technical Journal papers on "Rice Variety MR297 Fertilizer Interaction" and crop statistics from the Department of Agriculture Malaysia (DOA).

Mathematical Functions

Total Crop Yield Function $f(x)$
f(x) = -0.00018x² + 0.054x + 3.2
Where $x$ = Nitrogen applied ($\text{kg/ha}$), $f(x)$ = Predicted rice yield ($\text{metric tonnes/ha}$).
Marginal Efficiency Rate $f'(x)$
f'(x) = -0.00036x + 0.054
Calculates the precise yield increase or decrease per additional $\text{kg}$ of nitrogen applied.
